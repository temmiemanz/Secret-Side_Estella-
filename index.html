Estella was born December 17th, one year into the apocalypse, to a family already struggling. They gave her up to improve her odds — and didn't disclose the illnesses running through their bloodline, afraid it would land her somewhere worse. The Jones family took her in. They were stable, reasonably kind, and completely unable to close the distance that grew between her and their other children. She never fit, and she never knew why.
The illnesses came quietly at first. She started working out obsessively to keep pace with everyone else, not knowing she was making some of it worse. By six, the guilt of being a burden had compounded into something she couldn't carry anymore. She ran. She found Odette not long after — or Odette found her. They became inseparable almost immediately. Estella never told her about any of it.
She watched the cult take hold of Odette and couldn't figure out how to stop it. She watched her friend kill people and looked the other way until she couldn't anymore. At 13, two cities away, she tried to end it. The gun jammed. On the way back she got jumped by scavengers who took everything. She bought a pistol and a shank and walked home. Found Odette. Said nothing.
Her heart started going wrong before 17. Palpitations, sharp chest pain — all of it quiet, all of it hidden. Then at 18 Odette ran away, and Estella's reason to keep going ran with her. She searched for a month. When she found her, Odette was different — but Estella didn't care what had changed. She just needed her back. She still does. She just hasn't said it yet.
