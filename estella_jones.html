Abilities
passive Kinetic Storage
All force and damage received is halved. The remainder converts into Volts stored in the body, building from 1 through 5. Each Volt level unlocks a new capability.
Volt 1Enhanced strength and speed — body faintly crackles
Volt 2Electrically charged strikes — anything hit is shocked
Volt 3Short range electrical burst — repels anything close
Volt 4Conducted through surfaces — electrifies anything touched
Volt 5Fires concentrated electrical projectiles at range
active Discharge
Using any Volt level ability expels all stored Volts at once.
Limits
Volts do not decay — but using any ability resets storage to zero.
passive Overcharge
Taking damage at Volt 5 still absorbs as normal, but the excess discharges instantly back at the attacker at 1.5× the original force.
known bonus Heartbeat Trickle
The passive force of her own heartbeat feeds a constant trickle of Volts. She is always at Volt 1 — and her heart cannot fail naturally. Discovered as a direct result of her condition.
Lore
Estella was born December 17th, one year into the apocalypse, to a family already struggling under the weight of a collapsing world. They gave her up to improve her odds — and didn't disclose the illnesses running through their bloodline, afraid it would land her somewhere worse. The Jones family took her in. They were stable, reasonably kind, and completely unable to close the distance that grew between her and their other children. She never fit, and she never knew why.
The illnesses came quietly at first. Slower steps, more breaks, conversations that took longer to parse. She started working out obsessively to keep pace with everyone else, not knowing she was making some of it worse. By six, the guilt of being a burden had compounded into something she couldn't carry anymore. She ran.
She found Odette not long after — or Odette found her, depending on who's telling it. They became inseparable almost immediately. Estella never told her about any of it. Not the anemia, not the knees, not the back. She pushed herself to exhaustion every single day just to seem fine. She watched the cult take hold of Odette and couldn't figure out how to stop it. She watched her friend kill people and looked the other way until she couldn't anymore.
At 13, two cities away, she tried to end it. The gun jammed. She was furious — couldn't even do that right — and on her way back got jumped by scavengers who took everything except the will to go back. She bought a pistol and a shank and walked home. Found Odette. Said nothing. The bruises darkened and she pretended they weren't there.
Her heart started going wrong before 17. Palpitations. Sharp chest pain. All of it quiet, all of it hidden. Then at 18 Odette ran away, and Estella's reason to keep going ran with her. She searched for a month. When she found her, Odette was different — but Estella didn't care what had changed. She just needed her back. She still does. She just hasn't said it yet.
